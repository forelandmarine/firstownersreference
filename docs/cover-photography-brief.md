# Cover photography brief, 1st Edition

**Engagement window:** commission June 2026. Shoot July or early August 2026. Delivery for print August 2026.

## What the publication is

The First Owner's Reference is an annual editorial publication for first-time superyacht buyers, published by the independent consultancy Foreland Marine. The voice is calm, evidence-led, declarative, and structurally independent of yards, brokers, and management companies. It carries no advertising. It is sold by subscription and complimentary to confirmed buyers.

## What the cover is for

A first-time superyacht buyer typically sits on USD 20 to 500 million of recent liquidity and is months into a decision they have no prior experience of. The cover is the first object that signals the publication is serious, independent, and on their side of the table. It must read as a credible reference object, not a brochure.

For comparison: the visual register of WSJ Magazine, Monocle Quality of Life, Apollo, Apartamento. Restrained, considered, expensive. Not BOAT International, not SuperYacht World, not the trade press.

## What we are looking for

A single still image that does narrative work. The image is the cover. There is no other supporting illustration. The wordmark, strap, and publisher line sit over it as a small typographic intervention. The image must therefore carry the editorial weight on its own.

### What works

- A yacht in a moment of considered relationship to a place. A J-class at the head of a Mediterranean port at dawn. A 50 m moored alone in a Norwegian fjord. A motor yacht in transit through a quiet European canal. A hull on the slip at a Northern European yard, scaffolded, mid-refit.
- Architecture or infrastructure that frames a yacht in scale. La Ciotat's industrial gantry from below. The Lürssen Lemwerder slip from the water. Hamburg drydock with a hull dwarfed by surrounding cranes.
- Stillness, weight, scale. A yacht photographed from a height that emphasises proportion against landscape. A yacht photographed at distance that emphasises restraint.
- A trade detail at very close range. A welder's hood reflected in a cleaned-up hull plate. A varnished caprail and a coil of warps. A binnacle. A teak deck wet from a sluice-down. The image must read as competent, not romantic.

### What does not work

- Marketing wallpaper. A yacht against a sunset. A yacht on a perfect-water Mediterranean afternoon with a deckhand in white on the foredeck.
- Aspirational tropes. Champagne on the aft deck. A linen-clad couple. A barefoot owner at the wheel.
- Aerial drone shots where the yacht looks like a CGI render. Drone is acceptable only if the angle reveals scale or context the eye-level shot cannot.
- Anything that reads as a charter listing.
- Anything taken at a boat show.

## Technical requirements

- Format: portrait, 230 by 300 mm at trim plus 3 mm bleed each edge, so a final 236 by 306 mm at minimum.
- Resolution: 300 dpi at print size, so a minimum 2,795 by 3,614 px. Higher is welcome.
- Colour space: shoot RAW. Deliver TIFF or PSD, 16-bit, AdobeRGB. We convert to CMYK Fogra39 at the press file stage.
- Type-safe areas: the wordmark sits in the lower-left third of the cover. Leave a clean, low-contrast area approximately 120 by 90 mm in the lower-left for type to overlay without scrim. The strap sits below the wordmark and needs a further 30 mm horizontal band of low contrast.
- A second top-left area, approximately 40 by 12 mm, needs to be clean for the edition mark.
- The image should have natural contrast variety so a single type colour (paper, `#f5f2ec`) reads cleanly without a graphic scrim.

## Treatment and processing

- Colour grade should be quiet. Cool whites, deep marines, restrained warmth. No saturated marketing palette.
- Grain is acceptable and welcome on the right subject. Film capture is welcome.
- No over-sharpening. No HDR. No bracketed-and-fused look.
- Editorial style for retouching: clean, but lived. Remove production hardware that would distract; retain weathering, paint marks, and incidental presence of crew.

## Crew and access

If the shot requires access to a yacht, the publication has working relationships with several yards and management companies that can introduce access. Photographer to liaise with the editor (`editors@firstownersreference.com`) two weeks before the planned shoot date.

If the shot is on a private yacht, the owner must sign a release. The publication operates on the basis that the yacht is not identified by name on the cover or in any caption.

## Deliverables

- Final cover image as TIFF, 16-bit, AdobeRGB, 300 dpi at trim + 3 mm bleed.
- Two alternative crops or compositions from the same shoot in case the primary needs revision at proofing.
- A set of 10 to 20 supporting images from the shoot for potential use elsewhere in the publication (interior pages, supporting figures, the closing image at page 99) or for the digital edition.
- Caption text for each delivered image: location, date, subject, any contextual information the editor should know.
- Image release from any yacht or yard featured.

## Selection criteria

A successful cover photograph satisfies all of the following:

1. It does narrative work, not decoration. The reader, holding the magazine in hand, would pause on it.
2. It places a yacht in a relationship to scale, place, work, or restraint.
3. It carries an evident craft quality: composition, light, restraint, no marketing tropes.
4. It accepts the wordmark gracefully without competing with it.
5. It reads as a publication object, not a magazine cover.
6. The editor and the picture editor agree without reservation.
