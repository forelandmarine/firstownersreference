# Social media strategy

The First Owner's Reference, 1st Edition. Drafted May 2026 for hard launch ahead of Monaco Yacht Show, September 2026.

## Position

The publication is a buyer-protective editorial product, independent of yards, brokers, and management companies. Social media is an extension of the editorial position, not a marketing layer on top of it. Every post is a small piece of the publication. Source-cited, plain, declarative. No nautical romance. No broker or yard tags. No paid amplification. No partnerships.

The objective is not follower count. The objective is to put a calm, source-cited voice in front of three audiences (newly-liquid principals, family-office advisers, lawyers and wealth managers) who already use LinkedIn daily and who already see the trade-press feed and find it thin.

## Channels and roles

### LinkedIn (primary)

Three accounts post in coordination:

1. **The First Owner's Reference** (company page). The publication's own page. Editorial voice. 4 posts per week.
2. **Jack MacNally** (personal). Co-editor in chief. 2 to 3 posts per week, in his own register but on publication topics. Highest-leverage account because LinkedIn rewards individuals over pages.
3. **Daniel Marks** (personal). Co-editor in chief. 2 to 3 posts per week. Different register from Jack so the two voices read as distinct.

Foreland Marine's company page amplifies the publication page where editorially relevant (not every post). Velema, Inglis, Hooning, and other named contributors get tagged only when the post is about their published work or interview.

LinkedIn is the channel where readers are converted. Every post carries a single conversion point: the running cost calculator, the chapter, the print edition request, or a specific data spread.

### Instagram (secondary)

Single account: **@firstownersreference**. Calm, type-led, deliberately counter-aesthetic against yacht-Instagram. The job is to drive traffic to firstownersreference.com, not to compete on yacht imagery.

3 posts per week. Carousel-led. Quote-and-source format. No drone shots. No broker tags. No yard tags. No champagne. No sunsets.

Instagram is structurally a follower channel for the publication. LinkedIn does the conversion work.

## Voice and tone

Per the publication's editorial register.

- Plain, declarative, British English.
- No em-dashes. Use commas, periods, or rephrase.
- No nautical romance. No "set sail", "embark on", "your dream yacht".
- Italics for yacht names. Numbers in figures past nine.
- Sources named in the post body, not just hashtagged.
- No engagement bait ("agree?", "thoughts?"). The post earns its response or it does not.
- No emoji.
- Hashtags used sparingly, only where they aid discovery. Three or fewer per post.

## Visual system

In-house production. Foreland's design tokens reused so the social system is the publication's system at smaller scale.

- **Palette.** Paper #f5f2ec, charcoal #1a1a1a, marine #0f3b5c, stone #7a756d. Four colours only.
- **Type.** Newsreader serif for body and headlines. Geist sans for meta and labels. Geist Mono for chart labels and source attribution.
- **Templates.** A small set of reusable cards: data card (single statistic plus source), quote card (sentence from chapter plus chapter reference), correction card ("the trade press said X. The data says Y"), source card (chapter source list, end of carousel).
- **Sizes.** LinkedIn 1200 × 1200 (1:1) and 1080 × 1350 (4:5). Instagram 1080 × 1350 (4:5). Carousels of 4 to 8 cards. Always end with a source card and a small wordmark.
- **No drone yacht photography.** If yacht imagery is needed, Fraser Edwards' work or stock with clear rights. Default to type and chart, not photography.

## Content pillars

Five pillars. Each post sits in one. The mix across a week is roughly 2 / 1 / 1 / 1 / 1.

1. **Cited claim** (40 percent). A single number or finding from the publication, attributed to its source. Knight Frank Wealth Report 2026: $8.5bn yacht transactions, 70 percent YoY rise. BOAT International: 1,093 yachts on order, 568 of them in Italy. Quay Crew: captain salaries up 7 percent in the 70 to 79m bracket. Each post: claim, source, year, link to the chapter where the figure sits in context.
2. **Editorial position** (20 percent). The independence test. The 10 percent folklore. The dual-agency reality. The one or two paragraphs from the chapter that hold the position, posted as a card or quote. Not a sales pitch; the position itself.
3. **Behind the page** (20 percent). What the editorial team found while writing the chapter. The disagreement we surfaced between sources. The figure we could not verify. The thing the trade press got wrong. Builds the methodology argument and creates inbound contributor inquiries.
4. **Interview clip** (10 percent, when interviews land). Velema, Inglis, Hooning, Assael, Lay, others. 30 to 90 second video clips with subtitles. Plus a quote card pulled from the same interview. Held back until the interview is recorded; the social calendar is not dependent on this pillar.
5. **Reader and correspondence** (10 percent). Anonymised reader letters and questions. Reactions to specific chapters. The one calculator output that surprised the user. Not testimonial; reading-public dialogue.

## KPIs and what we don't measure

Measured weekly:

- **Print copy requests.** The conversion that matters. Track source per request (LinkedIn organic, Instagram organic, direct, referral).
- **Calculator runs.** Distinct sessions on /tools/running-cost-calculator. Stratify by social-referred and direct.
- **Chapter reads.** Sessions per chapter slug, time on page, scroll depth.
- **Inbound interview and contributor inquiries.** Email count per week into editors@firstownersreference.com tagged by referral source.
- **Citations from named industry voices.** Manual count: when SuperYacht Times, BOAT International, Megayacht News, Quay Crew, Pantaenius, or a yacht law firm cites the publication in their own content. This is the long-term legitimacy metric.

Not measured:

- Follower count as a primary metric. Reported but not steered toward.
- Likes or reaction count. Reported but not steered toward.
- Reach or impressions. Available in platform analytics; not our number.
- Engagement rate. Same.

## Independence guardrails

These are non-negotiable. Drift on any one undermines the editorial wedge.

- **No broker or yard tags** in posts. Not in body, not in alt text, not in image overlays. The only exception is source attribution (e.g. tagging Knight Frank when posting their data, which is a citation, not promotion).
- **No paid amplification.** No boosted posts. No LinkedIn ads. No Instagram ads. The strategy is organic by structural choice, not budget.
- **No partnerships, sponsorships, or affiliate arrangements.** If approached, the answer is no. This is stated on the colophon and is repeated when asked.
- **No product placement.** Yachts in posts are illustrative only. No featured-yacht-of-the-week. No yard-of-the-month.
- **Disclosure on every chart.** Source name plus year plus link to the chapter where the methodology sits.
- **No commission on links.** No affiliate URLs. No tracked partner links.
- **No engagement bait.** No "comment yes if you agree". No false-binary polls. The post earns response or it does not.
- **No yacht-Instagram aesthetic.** No drone photography. No champagne. No sunsets. No "yacht-life" captions.
- **No comment on individuals' personal lives.** Industry figures are discussed on their published positions, not their private affairs.
- **Crisis posture.** If criticised by a brokerage, yard, or trade publication, respond once, in writing, on the publication site, and link from social. Do not engage in social-thread back-and-forth.

## Operations

In-house production. No agency. Minimum budget.

- **Editorial calendar:** maintained as a separate file, /Users/jack/firstownersreference/docs/social-calendar.md, with one row per scheduled post (date, channel, account, pillar, asset reference, source citation).
- **Asset production:** Foreland-internal. Templates built in Figma against the publication's design tokens. Export to PNG.
- **Scheduling:** native LinkedIn and Instagram schedulers. No paid third-party tool.
- **Approvals:** Jack approves all publication-page posts before they go out. Daniel approves Jack's posts and vice versa as a sanity check. Personal posts do not require approval.
- **Reporting:** weekly tally on the five measured KPIs above. One Friday email to Jack and Daniel.
- **Time commitment:** ~3 hours per week of writing per account. ~2 hours per week of design production. ~1 hour per week of scheduling and reporting. Total ~12 to 15 hours across the team per week.

## What we don't do

- We do not run giveaways or competitions.
- We do not do "yacht-of-the-week" content or any content where a specific commercial vessel is celebrated.
- We do not interview brokers about their listings.
- We do not republish broker press releases.
- We do not chase trending audio or yacht-TikTok formats.
- We do not run user-generated content campaigns. The publication's voice is the voice.
- We do not buy followers, likes, or engagement.
- We do not enter into any commercial relationship that creates a contingent-fee tie to any party we would write about.

## Channels not in this strategy

- **TikTok.** Wrong audience for the publication. UHNW principals and family-office advisers do not consume yacht-buying decision-grade material on TikTok.
- **YouTube.** Considered for interview clips, but not the primary distribution. Clips will live on the publication site with shareable timestamps. YouTube comes later if the clip library scales.
- **X / Twitter.** The audience exists but the platform's tone is hostile to the publication's register. Not pursued for 1st Edition.
- **Substack / newsletter.** Considered separately. The quarterly digital supplements (January, April, July, October) are the publication's serial format. A newsletter would compete with them. Revisit for 2nd Edition.
- **Threads, Bluesky, Mastodon.** Not pursued. The audience is not there.
