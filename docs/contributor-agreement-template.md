## 1. Contribution and grant

1.1 The Contributor will provide an editorial contribution (the "Contribution") to *The First Owner's Reference* in the form of a written piece, an interview, a quoted statement, or a guest opinion, as agreed in writing in advance.

1.2 The Contributor grants the Publisher a non-exclusive, perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free licence to publish the Contribution in print and on the website at firstownersreference.com, in archives, in derivative editorial formats, and in commercial promotional material relating to the publication. The Contributor retains copyright in the underlying work.

## 2. No fee

2.1 The Contribution is given voluntarily. The Publisher pays no fee, royalty, retainer, or other consideration for the Contribution. This is the policy across the publication and is disclosed in the colophon.

2.2 Nothing in this agreement creates an employment, agency, partnership, or service relationship between the parties.

## 3. Originality and authority

3.1 The Contributor warrants that the Contribution is the Contributor's own work, that the Contributor has the authority to grant the licence in clause 1.2, and that the Contribution does not knowingly infringe any third party's copyright, defamation, privacy, or confidentiality rights.

3.2 The Contributor warrants that any factual claims made in the Contribution are made in good faith and to the best of the Contributor's knowledge at the time of submission.

## 4. Editorial control

4.1 The Publisher retains final editorial control. Light typographic and stylistic editing will be applied without further consultation. Any material edit (a change of meaning, a deletion of substantive content, or a rewriting of a quoted passage) will be agreed with the Contributor in writing before publication.

4.2 The Publisher reserves the right to decline to publish the Contribution at its sole discretion, without obligation to provide reasons.

## 5. Confidentiality and data sharing

5.1 The Contributor may share data, project files, market intelligence, or other proprietary material with the Publisher in support of the Contribution. Any such data will be:
  (a) used only in connection with the Contribution and any directly related editorial follow-up;
  (b) anonymised to the standard set out in the Publisher's anonymisation protocol where the data identifies a third party;
  (c) not on-shared to a third party without the Contributor's written consent.

5.2 The Publisher commits to applying the anonymisation protocol to any composite case material before publication, with named-firm references reviewed under the Publisher's named-firm review standard.

## 6. Disclosure

6.1 The Contributor will disclose to the Publisher, before publication, any commercial relationship between the Contributor (or their firm) and the named subjects, parties, or counterparties referred to in the Contribution.

6.2 Material disclosures will be published alongside the Contribution. Where there is no relevant disclosure, that fact will be stated.

## 7. Withdrawal and correction

7.1 The Contributor may withdraw the Contribution at any time before publication by written notice. After publication, the Contribution will remain in the published edition; the Publisher will record any post-publication request for correction or amplification and consider it on its merits.

7.2 The Publisher will issue published corrections where a factual error is identified, on the corrections page of firstownersreference.com.

## 8. Use of name and affiliation

8.1 The Publisher may use the Contributor's name, role, and firm affiliation in connection with the Contribution: in the print edition, on the website, in social media promotion of the Contribution, and in editorial reference to the Contribution in subsequent editions.

8.2 The Publisher may not use the Contributor's name in a way that suggests endorsement of the Publisher's commercial consultancy services without the Contributor's separate written consent.

## 9. Term and termination

9.1 This agreement is effective on signature and continues for the lifetime of the publication, subject to clauses 7 and 11.

## 10. Governing law

10.1 This agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising from it.

## 11. Entire agreement

11.1 This agreement contains the entire agreement between the parties on the subject matter and supersedes any prior understanding.

## Editorial notes (not part of the agreement)

- Clause 5 anchors data-sharing terms; once the Foreland archive intelligence layer goes live, this will be referenced in commissioning briefs to set expectations early.
- Clause 6 makes disclosure a contractual obligation, not just an editorial preference. Aligns with the independence test logic across the publication.
- Clause 8.2 protects contributors from being co-opted into Foreland's commercial brand, which matters once the publication's identity is being separated from Foreland's.
- Once retained media counsel reviews, expected redlines are likely on the perpetual licence (may need narrower scope or an early-termination clause), and on the warranty in clause 3.1 (counsel may want a knowledge qualifier or limit of liability).
