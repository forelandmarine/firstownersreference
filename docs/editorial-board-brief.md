# Editorial board brief

The 1st Edition currently lists "Editorial board: To be confirmed" in the masthead. This is honest but it is also the single most visible credibility gap in the proof. A publishing house, a confirmed contributor, or a discerning reader who picks the publication up at a yacht show will read TBC and form a view about how finished the editorial proposition is.

This brief lists candidates for a three- to five-member editorial board to be confirmed before the press file goes to the printer in late August 2026.

## What the board is for

The editorial board exists to do three things.

1. **Source independence.** The board's named presence is the structural guarantee that the publication is not a Foreland Marine vehicle. The board is what makes the independence claim verifiable.
2. **Editorial pressure.** Board members read draft chapters in confidence and push back where the argument is weak, the sourcing is incomplete, or the editorial line is unfair to a named party.
3. **Reader-side legitimacy.** A reader who recognises a board name and respects them is more likely to trust the publication's editorial conclusions.

## What the board is not

The board is not an advisory council. It does not set strategy, does not approve commissioning, does not vote on chapters. Editorial control sits with the co-editors-in-chief. Board members are credited and listed; their function is structural credibility and editorial pressure, not governance.

## What we ask of board members

- Read each annual edition in draft, 4 to 6 weeks before press, with one round of written notes returned to the editors.
- Be listed in the masthead with name, professional affiliation, and the chapter (or chapters) of nominal oversight.
- Disagreement is welcomed. Public statements distancing from a specific editorial conclusion are accepted in writing and reflected in subsequent editions.
- One annual board meeting, in person or remote, 90 minutes, agenda set by the editors.

## What we do not ask

- No fee. Membership is voluntary, consistent with the contributor model. Travel and meal expenses for the annual board meeting are reimbursed.
- No commercial relationship with Foreland Marine. Board members must not be current Foreland clients, current Foreland counterparties, or operating in a capacity that would create a continuing commercial flow with Foreland.
- No representation. Board members are not asked to speak on behalf of the publication in public except where they choose to.

### Outreach principle

The publication's value proposition to a board member is two things: a credible new title that has done the editorial work to deserve their name; and a structural position in the trade press that nobody else is occupying. Both are real. The pitch is short:

> "We are launching The First Owner's Reference, an annual editorial publication for first-time superyacht buyers, in September 2026. The structural argument is that the trade press is captured by the firms it covers, and that a first-time buyer deserves a calm, evidence-led reference written from the other side of the table. We are assembling an editorial board of three to five named external readers who will see drafts before press, push back where the argument is weak, and be credited in the masthead. The commitment is one annual edition in draft, one round of written notes, one 90-minute meeting. Membership is unpaid; expenses are reimbursed. No commercial relationship with Foreland Marine is required or permitted during board service. We would like to discuss your participation."

### Timeline

- **2026-06-01 to 2026-06-30**: outreach in batches of three. Editors meet (in person or remote) with willing candidates.
- **2026-07-01 to 2026-07-15**: confirm three to five members. Sign a one-page board member letter with the publication's commitments and expectations.
- **2026-07-20**: draft of full publication shared with confirmed board members.
- **2026-08-15**: board notes returned. Editors incorporate where they agree.
- **2026-08-25**: press file packaged with confirmed board listed in masthead.

### What if we cannot confirm three by July?

If we cannot confirm three named members by mid-July, the publication has three options.

1. Postpone the September 2026 launch to a later date when the board is real.
2. Launch with one or two named members and a clear statement that the board is being assembled.
3. Drop the "Editorial board" field from the masthead entirely and acknowledge in the colophon that the editorial structure is currently the co-editors plus an external annual readers' pass.

Option 3 is the cleanest fallback. Option 1 is the most ambitious. Option 2 carries a credibility cost smaller than TBC but greater than option 3.

## What this brief does not address

- Specific names of editorial board candidates beyond the starting set above. The editor should add 8 to 10 more candidates after a round of consultation with industry contacts.
- Compensation. The board model is unpaid by design, in line with the contributor model. If a candidate raises compensation, the conversation is over and the candidate is not invited.
- Conflicts that emerge after board service starts. The board letter should commit board members to disclose any new commercial relationship with Foreland Marine within 30 days and resign the position if the relationship is material.
- Term length. The editorial board is renewed annually with each edition. A board member can serve consecutive years subject to the editors' agreement.
